Fund Performance Assessment

FY2026 is SPRING's strongest year to date — a +43.29% Class I return against Nasdaq's +25.60% is a compelling 17.7-point beat. The $1.874B in unrealized gains, combined with zero material detractors across 419 positions, confirms the portfolio's private market investments broadly appreciated. The fund's secondary-focused strategy continues generating alpha by purchasing positions at discounts to fair value. Rapid AUM growth (from $1.7B to $6.5B in one year) is a testament to investor demand but also creates deployment pressure — maintaining return quality across a 3.8× larger capital base is the primary operational challenge ahead.

Impact on Your $250,000 Class S Investment

  • FY2026 estimated gain on $250K: ~+$105,325 — applying +42.13% Class S return
  • Class S NAV now $61.29 — up from the $59.79 used in prior projections (+$1.50/share, +2.5% additional gain not yet reflected)
  • SpaceX 20.2% exposure confirmed — consistent with Holdings Analysis; IPO boost estimate unchanged
  • Base CAGR consideration: The 42.13% FY2026 return far exceeds the 26.05% 3-year CAGR used in projections — consider adjusting the Base Case dropdown upward for a more optimistic scenario, while keeping 26% as the conservative anchor
  • Early repurchase fee risk: Shares held under one year face a 2.00% early redemption penalty — important given your redemption planning

Opportunities

  • SpaceX IPO catalyst intact: Audited financials confirm 20.2% NAV exposure — a mid-2026 IPO at $1.5T would add ~$22,700 to your $250K in Year 1
  • Post-period momentum: $867.6M in subscriptions in just April–May 2026 signals accelerating institutional adoption, supporting ongoing secondary market deal flow
  • Databricks pipeline: $128.5M invested across tranches; a Databricks IPO would be a second major catalyst independent of SpaceX
  • Mizuho credit facility ($350M): More than doubles borrowing capacity — enables faster deployment of $416.7M unfunded commitments at favorable pricing

Risks & Watch Items

  • Expense ratio (10.03% gross): Class S carries a 0.85% distribution fee on top of management and incentive fees — significant cost drag compounding over time
  • Early repurchase fee (2.00%): Applies to shares held under one year — a material penalty for any investor considering near-term partial redemption
  • $416.7M unfunded commitments: Contractually obligated future capital calls may require credit line drawdowns or constrain new investments
  • Deployment pressure: Absorbing $867.6M in new April/May subscriptions while maintaining return quality across 419+ positions is a meaningful execution risk
  • Tax deferral ($93.1M ordinary loss carryforward): May result in future taxable distributions — worth monitoring for Class S investors in taxable accounts

Bottom Line

This annual report is strongly positive for your $250,000 Class S position. The EY-audited +42.13% FY2026 return, confirmed $6.489B NAV, $1.874B unrealized gains, and $867.6M in post-period subscriptions all signal a fund executing well above benchmark with growing institutional confidence. The NAV update to $61.29 (vs. $59.79 in prior projections) is a minor upward revision to your position value. The primary consideration is whether to adjust the Projections tab Base Case upward from 26.05% to reflect the stronger realized FY2026 return — though maintaining the conservative 3-year CAGR as the anchor remains defensible.
